# Key Terms

## AML

Anti-Money Laundering. A set of controls and processes used to detect and prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crime risks.

## KYC

Know Your Customer. The process of identifying and verifying a customer, understanding their profile, and assessing their risk level.

## CDD

Customer Due Diligence. The standard review process used to understand a customer’s identity, activity, purpose, and risk profile.

## EDD

Enhanced Due Diligence. A deeper review applied to higher-risk customers, transactions, or relationships.

## PEP

Politically Exposed Person. A person who holds, or has held, a prominent public position and may require additional risk review.

## Sanctions Screening

The process of checking customers, entities, or transactions against sanctions lists to identify possible restrictions or legal risks.

## Adverse Media

Negative news or public information that may indicate reputational, financial crime, fraud, corruption, or compliance risk.

## Fraud Risk

The risk that an account, transaction, identity, or activity may involve deception, unauthorized access, or financial loss.

## Account Takeover

A situation where an unauthorized person gains access to an account and may attempt to change account details, move funds, or perform suspicious activity.

## Transaction Monitoring

The process of reviewing transaction activity to identify unusual, suspicious, or high-risk patterns.

## Risk Indicator

A specific sign or pattern that may suggest increased risk and requires further review.

## False Positive

A case or alert that initially appears risky but is later determined not to represent actual suspicious activity.

## Escalation

The process of sending a case to a senior analyst, specialist team, or higher review level when the risk cannot be closed at the first review stage.

## Case Documentation

A written record of what was reviewed, what evidence was found, what risk was identified, and what action was recommended.

## Due Diligence

A structured review process used to understand a person, company, transaction, or relationship before making a risk-based decision.

## Compliance Research

Research conducted to identify legal, regulatory, reputational, or financial crime risks.

## Source Review

The process of checking information from documents, databases, media sources, or internal records to support a risk assessment.

## Risk Assessment

A structured judgment about the level of risk based on evidence, context, and observed activity.

## Suspicious Activity

Activity that appears unusual, inconsistent, unauthorized, or potentially linked to fraud, financial crime, or other risk.
