# Case 005 - Cash-Intensive Business Review

## Case Type

Simulated STR and risk operations case

## Summary

This case reviews a simulated suspicious transaction reporting scenario involving a cash-intensive business and repeated cash deposits.

The purpose of this case is to practice STR review, customer profile comparison, hypothesis building, evidence review, and escalation-quality reporting.

## Scenario

A customer who owns a small cafe made repeated cash deposits after weekend business activity. The deposits were reported for review because the cash amounts appeared significant and similar activity had been observed before.

The customer’s business type may explain regular cash deposits, as cafes can operate as cash-intensive businesses. However, the analyst should still review whether the amount, frequency, pattern, and source of funds are consistent with the customer’s business profile.

- Available source of funds information
- Business records or supporting information if available

## Hypothesis Building

A suspicious transaction report should not be treated as proof of wrongdoing. It should be used as a starting point for analysis.

### Possible Legitimate Hypothesis

The cash deposits may originate from normal weekend cafe sales. If the amount and frequency are consistent with the customer’s business profile, the activity may be explainable.

### Possible Suspicious Hypothesis

The cash deposits may indicate possible structuring or laundering through a cash-intensive business if the amounts, timing, frequency, or account activity are inconsistent with the customer profile or expected business turnover.

## Risk Assessment

The activity should be reviewed against the customer’s business profile and expected cash turnover. A cafe may legitimately generate cash income, so cash deposits are not suspicious by themselves.

However, repeated cash deposits may require further review if they are unusually large, structured, inconsistent with the stated business activity, or followed by rapid movement of funds.

Previous STRs or similar reports may increase the need for review, but they do not prove suspicious activity by themselves.

## Recommended Action

- Compare the deposit amounts with the expected business profile
- Review deposit frequency and timing
- Check whether the deposits are consistent with weekend business activity
- Review previous STRs or related reports
- Check account activity before and after the deposits
- Review source of funds information if available
- Escalate if the activity remains unexplained or inconsistent with the business profile

## Escalation Note

Potential cash-intensive business review identified. The customer owns a cafe and made repeated cash deposits after weekend activity. Cash deposits may be consistent with normal cafe operations; however, the amount, frequency, and pattern should be reviewed against the customer’s business profile and expected turnover. Previous similar reports may indicate a repeated pattern, but they do not confirm suspicious activity by themselves. The case may require escalation if the deposits remain unexplained or inconsistent with the available evidence.

## Analyst Reflection

This case demonstrates that an STR is not proof of wrongdoing. It is a starting point for structured analysis.

A cash-intensive business may have a legitimate explanation for repeated cash deposits. The analyst should avoid assuming guilt and should test both legitimate and suspicious hypotheses using available evidence, customer profile information, and transaction history.
